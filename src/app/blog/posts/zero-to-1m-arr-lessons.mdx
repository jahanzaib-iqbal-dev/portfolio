## The Beginning

In early 2022, I joined Obenan as the first technical hire. There was no codebase, no architecture, no team — just a clear vision from the founders and a market opportunity in local SEO.

Four years later, the platform serves thousands of businesses, generates over $1M in annual recurring revenue, and runs on an architecture that has scaled without a single major rewrite.

Here's what I learned along the way.

## Architectural Decisions That Paid Off

### 1. Build for Production from Day One

The temptation with MVPs is to cut corners — "we'll fix it later." We didn't. Every database migration was backward-compatible. Every API endpoint was versioned. Every service had proper error handling.

This wasn't slower. It was actually faster in the long run because we never had to stop and rewrite.

### 2. PostgreSQL Over Everything

We chose PostgreSQL early and leaned into it heavily — JSONB columns for flexible schemas, proper indexing strategies, and materialized views for analytics. When NoSQL was trendy, we stuck with what we knew would scale reliably.

### 3. Invest in CI/CD Early

Automated testing and deployment pipelines were set up in the first month. This paid dividends every single day after that. By the time we had a team, the deployment process was already battle-tested.

## Leadership Lessons

### Hire for Ownership, Not Just Skill

The engineers who made the biggest impact weren't necessarily the most experienced — they were the ones who treated the product like their own. Ownership mindset beats technical skill every time.

### Code Reviews Are Culture

We established code review as a non-negotiable practice from day one. It wasn't about gatekeeping — it was about knowledge sharing. Every PR was a teaching moment.

### Ship Weekly, Demo Weekly

We maintained a weekly demo cadence with the founders throughout the entire journey. This kept alignment tight and feedback loops short. No feature ever went more than a week without being seen by stakeholders.

## The Numbers

- **0 to $1M+ ARR** in under 4 years
- **Zero major rewrites** — the original architecture scaled
- **Team grew from 1 to 8** engineers under my leadership
- **Thousands of businesses** actively using the platform

## What I'd Do Differently

Honestly? Not much. But if I had to pick one thing: I'd invest in observability earlier. We added proper logging and monitoring about 6 months in. Those first 6 months of debugging in the dark were unnecessarily painful.

## The Takeaway

Building a product from zero to $1M+ ARR taught me that the hardest problems aren't technical — they're about making the right decisions at the right time with incomplete information. The code is the easy part. The judgment is what matters.
